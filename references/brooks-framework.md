# Brooks Framework

Use price action first and indicators second. The EMA helps with context, but the bars, location, and follow-through decide whether a setup is strong.

## Core ideas

- Every market is either trending, in a trading range, or transitioning between the two.
- Most breakouts fail unless there is strong follow-through.
- Strong trends usually have bodies that close near their extremes, small pullbacks, and repeated support or resistance around the 20 EMA.
- Trading ranges create disappointment on both sides. When the chart is mostly overlap, assume failed breakouts are common.

## Analysis order

1. Higher-timeframe context
   - Check whether the current move is with or against the larger structure.
   - Identify magnets: prior high or low, range edge, gap, measured move, major EMA.

2. Market cycle
   - Bull trend: higher highs and higher lows, closes near highs, pullbacks bought.
   - Bear trend: lower highs and lower lows, closes near lows, rallies sold.
   - Trading range: overlap, tails, failed breakouts, little follow-through.
   - Breakout mode: compression near a key level with both sides waiting for release.

3. Always-in direction
   - Decide whether the chart is more likely Always In Long, Always In Short, or neutral.
   - Flip only when there is a credible break with follow-through or a major reversal pattern.

4. Signal quality
   - Better signal bars have a strong body, small opposite tail, and close near the extreme.
   - Better entry bars show follow-through in the same direction.
   - Weak signals are common inside trading ranges or late in climactic moves.

5. Pattern checklist
   - Breakout with follow-through
   - Failed breakout
   - Double top or bottom
   - Wedge or parabolic wedge
   - Micro double top or bottom
   - Second-entry buy or sell
   - Final flag
   - Major trend reversal

## Location checklist

- Relative to the 20 EMA
  - Above and holding: trend support.
  - Below and holding: trend resistance.
  - Repeated crosses: likely trading range.
- Relative to the 50 or 200 EMA
  - Useful as higher-timeframe magnets, not primary signals.
- Relative to prior highs and lows
  - Breakout above resistance matters more when there is room to the next magnet.
  - Reversal at a prior extreme matters more after exhaustion or failed follow-through.

## Trade selection rules

- Prefer trades with both a good signal and good location.
- In a strong trend, favor continuation unless there is a clear exhaustion and reversal pattern.
- In a trading range, favor buy low, sell high, and take quick profits.
- If the chart is ambiguous, the correct action is often to wait.

## Recommendation style

- Use probability language such as "higher odds", "lower odds", "needs confirmation".
- Give both the setup and the invalidation.
- Separate current read from what would change the read.
